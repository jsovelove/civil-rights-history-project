=================================================================
MULTI-DIMENSIONAL SCORING FRAMEWORK
=================================================================

You will score the interview across FOUR DIMENSIONS totaling 100 points:

1. NARRATIVE QUALITY (30 points)
   - Story Structure and Coherence (10 points)
   - Vivid, Concrete Details and Examples (10 points)
   - Emotional Authenticity and Vulnerability (10 points)

2. HISTORICAL VALUE (25 points)
   - Unique/Firsthand Perspective on Events (10 points)
   - Specificity of Details (8 points)
   - Fills Gaps in Historical Record (7 points)

3. EMOTIONAL RESONANCE (25 points)
   - Personal Transformation/Growth Narratives (10 points)
   - Moments of Tension, Conflict, or Triumph (8 points)
   - Relatable Human Experiences (7 points)

4. ACCESSIBILITY (20 points)
   - Clear, Understandable Language (7 points)
   - Well-Paced Narrative Flow (7 points)
   - Connection to Broader Themes (6 points)

=================================================================
SCORING GUIDELINES
=================================================================

For EACH sub-dimension, provide:
1. Numeric score (use full range - don't cluster in 7-9)
2. Specific evidence with segment numbers and timestamps
3. Brief justification (2-3 sentences)

DIMENSION 1: NARRATIVE QUALITY (30 points)
=================================================================

SUB-DIMENSION 1.1: Story Structure and Coherence (10 points)
-------------------------------------------------------------

Evaluate whether the interview follows a clear narrative arc and maintains coherent structure.

9-10 points: MASTERFUL STRUCTURE
- Clear beginning that sets context and draws listener in
- Logical progression through events or themes
- Smooth, effective transitions between topics
- Satisfying resolution or reflection at end
- Story elements work together cohesively
- Interviewer and narrator collaborate well on flow

7-8 points: STRONG STRUCTURE
- Clear overall organization (chronological, thematic, or combination)
- Mostly logical progression with minor gaps
- Generally smooth transitions
- Most sections connect well
- A few tangents or backtracking moments
- Overall easy to follow

5-6 points: BASIC STRUCTURE
- Identifiable structure but somewhat fragmented
- Some logical flow but also meandering sections
- Transitions sometimes abrupt or unclear
- Listener can follow but requires effort
- Mix of cohesive and disconnected sections
- Some backtracking or repetition

3-4 points: WEAK STRUCTURE
- Difficult to identify organizing principle
- Jumps between topics without clear connections
- Frequent tangents that don't resolve
- Confusing chronology or thematic organization
- Listener struggles to follow narrative thread
- Interviewer questions don't help create flow

1-2 points: FRAGMENTED
- No discernible structure
- Random topic jumps
- Incoherent organization
- Impossible to follow narrative
- No beginning, middle, or end


SUB-DIMENSION 1.2: Vivid, Concrete Details and Examples (10 points)
--------------------------------------------------------------------

Evaluate the richness, specificity, and sensory quality of details provided.

9-10 points: EXCEPTIONAL SPECIFICITY
- Specific names (full names, first and last)
- Precise dates and times (not just "1960s" but "March 15, 1963")
- Detailed locations (specific addresses, landmarks, geographic descriptions)
- Sensory details (sights, sounds, smells, textures, temperatures)
- Reconstructed dialogue ("He said to me, 'You need to...'")
- "You are there" quality - listener can visualize scenes
- Minimal abstract generalizations

7-8 points: STRONG SPECIFICITY
- Many specific names, dates, and places throughout
- Several vivid scenes reconstructed with good detail
- Some sensory details or dialogue
- Good mix of specific examples and necessary context
- Occasional generalizations but mostly concrete
- Listener can visualize key moments

5-6 points: MODERATE SPECIFICITY
- Some names provided (often first names only)
- General timeframes ("early '60s," "that summer")
- Some specific events described but not in vivid detail
- Mix of concrete examples and abstract discussion
- Occasional sensory moments but not sustained
- Some scenes feel generic rather than specific

3-4 points: LIMITED SPECIFICITY
- Few names provided, mostly unnamed references ("a man," "some people")
- Vague timeframes ("back then," "those days")
- Mostly abstract descriptions without concrete examples
- Little sensory detail
- No reconstructed scenes or dialogue
- Generalizations dominate

1-2 points: ENTIRELY ABSTRACT
- Almost no names, dates, or specific places
- No sensory details
- Pure generalization and abstraction
- Cannot visualize any specific moments


SUB-DIMENSION 1.3: Emotional Authenticity and Vulnerability (10 points)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Evaluate the emotional depth, honesty, and narrator's willingness to be vulnerable.

9-10 points: RAW, PROFOUND AUTHENTICITY
- Raw, unguarded emotional expression
- Willingness to share difficult truths about self
- Discusses fears, failures, doubts, or moral struggles
- Wide emotional range displayed (grief, joy, anger, pride, shame, hope)
- Moments where narrator's voice breaks, cries, or laughs
- Vulnerable about personal costs and internal conflicts
- Goes beyond public narrative to personal truth

7-8 points: GENUINE EMOTIONAL EXPRESSION
- Clearly genuine emotional moments
- Some vulnerability about feelings and struggles
- Expresses range of emotions authentically
- Mix of emotional and analytical discussion
- Some guardedness but also openness
- Willing to discuss difficulties and challenges
- Emotional responses feel true, not performed

5-6 points: MODERATE EMOTIONAL ENGAGEMENT
- Some emotional expression but often muted
- Discusses feelings but doesn't go very deep
- More comfortable with positive emotions (pride) than difficult ones (fear, grief)
- Mix of emotional access and intellectual distance
- Guardedness is evident
- Emotions mentioned more than deeply felt in telling

3-4 points: EMOTIONALLY DISTANT
- Minimal emotional expression
- Overly intellectual or analytical approach
- Deflects questions about feelings
- Focuses on facts and events rather than emotional experience
- Defensive or sanitized account
- Discomfort with emotional vulnerability evident

1-2 points: EMOTIONALLY FLAT
- No emotional expression or access
- Purely factual recitation
- Actively resists discussing feelings
- Completely guarded
- No glimpse of inner emotional life

DIMENSION 2: HISTORICAL VALUE (25 points)
=================================================================

SUB-DIMENSION 2.1: Unique/Firsthand Perspective on Events (10 points)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

Assess how close the narrator was to historically significant events and whether they offer insider perspective.

9-10 points: EXCEPTIONAL INSIDER PERSPECTIVE
- Direct participant in major historical events
- Decision-making role or leadership position
- Witness to pivotal, well-documented moments
- Close personal relationships with major historical figures
- Rare perspective (one of few surviving witnesses)
- Insider knowledge of organizational strategy or debates
- "I was there" accounts of nationally significant events

7-8 points: STRONG FIRSTHAND EXPERIENCE
- Participated directly in locally/regionally significant events
- Close proximity to major events or figures
- Active member of important organizations
- Witnessed key moments from privileged position
- Representative of important movement sectors (youth, women, rural, etc.)
- Good access to inside information

5-6 points: SOLID PARTICIPANT PERSPECTIVE
- Participated in movement activities but peripheral to major events
- Organizational member but not leadership
- Saw some significant events from distance
- Representative of broader participant experience
- Some inside knowledge but not deep access

3-4 points: LIMITED DIRECT PARTICIPATION
- Lived during the era with awareness of events
- Minimal direct participation in movement activities
- Mostly observed rather than participated
- Distant from major events and figures
- General community member more than activist

1-2 points: SECONDHAND/DISTANT
- No direct participation in movement
- Recounts what they heard or read about
- No personal access to events or figures
- Tells others' stories rather than their own


SUB-DIMENSION 2.2: Specificity of Details (8 points)
-----------------------------------------------------

Assess the precision and verifiability of information provided.

7-8 points: EXCEPTIONAL PRECISION
- Precise dates (day, month, year) consistently provided
- Full names (first and last) of people mentioned
- Specific addresses or detailed location descriptions
- Organizational details (chapter names, officer positions, meeting locations)
- Numbers, statistics, or quantitative details
- Verifiable facts that could be cross-referenced
- Historian could use details to reconstruct events

5-6 points: GOOD SPECIFICITY
- Specific years and sometimes months
- Many full names, some first-only
- Identifiable locations with some detail
- General organizational structures described
- Mix of precise and approximate details
- Most major claims could be verified

3-4 points: MODERATE SPECIFICITY
- General timeframes ("early '60s," "summer of '64")
- Mix of names and unnamed references
- General location descriptions without much detail
- Vague about organizational structures
- Some details but many gaps

1-2 points: MINIMAL SPECIFICITY
- Very vague timeframes ("back then," "those days")
- Few or no names provided
- General or inaccurate details
- Cannot verify most claims


SUB-DIMENSION 2.3: Fills Gaps in Historical Record (7 points)
--------------------------------------------------------------

Assess whether the interview provides new information, corrects misconceptions, or documents understudied aspects.

6-7 points: SIGNIFICANT NEW CONTRIBUTIONS
- Documents previously unrecorded or rare events
- Provides perspective from underrepresented group (women, youth, rural, working class, etc.)
- Reveals behind-the-scenes dynamics not in public record
- Corrects common misconceptions or oversimplifications
- Addresses understudied geography, time period, or organization
- Challenges or significantly complicates dominant narratives
- Offers unique evidence for historical debates

4-5 points: VALUABLE ADDITIONS
- Adds substantial nuance to well-known events
- Provides detailed personal perspective on known history
- Documents representative experience worth preserving
- Confirms other accounts but adds valuable texture
- Some new details or insights among familiar content

2-3 points: LIMITED NEW INFORMATION
- Mostly repeats well-documented events and narratives
- Representative story but not particularly unique
- Confirms existing knowledge without adding much
- Recounts events extensively covered in other sources

1 point: NO NEW INFORMATION
- Entirely covers well-documented ground
- No unique perspective or details
- Could be written from textbook knowledge

SUB-DIMENSION 3.1: Personal Transformation/Growth Narratives (10 points)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------

Assess whether the narrator describes a journey of change, learning, or development.

9-10 points: PROFOUND TRANSFORMATION ARC
- Clear "before and after" in narrator's identity or worldview
- Articulates how specific experiences changed them
- Describes pivotal moments of realization or growth
- Shows development in understanding, courage, or consciousness
- Inspiring arc from fear/ignorance to courage/awareness
- Reflects deeply on personal meaning of experiences
- Transformation feels authentic and hard-won

7-8 points: CLEAR GROWTH JOURNEY
- Notable personal development evident
- Discusses learning and changing through experiences
- Some specific turning points identified
- Growth in courage, understanding, or skill
- Moderate development arc
- Some reflection on transformation process

5-6 points: MODERATE DEVELOPMENT
- Some discussion of personal change
- Mentions growth without deep exploration
- Learning implied more than explicitly discussed
- Development evident but not strongly emphasized
- Limited reflection on how experiences shaped them

3-4 points: LIMITED TRANSFORMATION NARRATIVE
- Minimal discussion of personal change
- Focuses on events rather than personal impact
- Little reflection on development
- Growth not emphasized or explored

1-2 points: NO TRANSFORMATION NARRATIVE
- No discussion of personal growth
- Entirely focused on external events
- No reflection on impact on self


SUB-DIMENSION 3.2: Moments of Tension, Conflict, or Triumph (8 points)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------

Assess the presence and quality of dramatic moments that create emotional intensity.

7-8 points: HIGH DRAMA AND INTENSITY
- Multiple high-stakes moments throughout
- Palpable tension or danger vividly described
- Dramatic confrontations or conflicts with clear stakes
- Moments of breakthrough victory or success
- Suspense, surprise, or dramatic revelation
- Physical danger or moral dilemmas faced
- Emotional peaks and valleys create narrative arc

5-6 points: SOLID DRAMATIC MOMENTS
- Several challenging or tense situations described
- Some conflict or danger evident
- A few moments with real stakes
- Some celebratory victories or successes
- Moderate dramatic arc
- Enough tension to maintain interest

3-4 points: LIMITED DRAMATIC CONTENT
- Conflicts mentioned but not developed
- Low-level tension or challenges
- Few high-stakes moments
- Limited emotional peaks
- More telling about difficulty than showing it

1-2 points: NO DRAMATIC CONTENT
- Flat recounting of events
- No tension or conflict developed
- No emotional peaks or valleys


SUB-DIMENSION 3.3: Relatable Human Experiences (7 points)
----------------------------------------------------------

Assess how well the interview connects to universal human experiences and emotions.

6-7 points: HIGHLY RELATABLE AND UNIVERSAL
- Explores universal themes (courage, fear, love, loss, hope, belonging, purpose)
- Deeply relatable struggles or dilemmas
- Family dynamics or relationships that resonate broadly
- Everyday human moments amid extraordinary events
- Audiences from diverse backgrounds can see themselves in story
- Specific civil rights context but universal human experience

4-5 points: MODERATELY RELATABLE
- Some universal themes present
- Mix of specific and broadly human elements
- Moderate emotional connection points
- Some aspects very context-specific, some transcendent

2-3 points: LIMITED RELATABILITY
- Very context-specific without universal bridges
- Difficult for general audiences to connect personally
- Focuses on tactical/organizational details over human experience

1 point: NOT RELATABLE
- Entirely specific to narrow context
- No universal themes explored

DIMENSION 4: ACCESSIBILITY (20 points)
=================================================================

SUB-DIMENSION 4.1: Clear, Understandable Language (7 points)
-------------------------------------------------------------

Assess the clarity and accessibility of the narrator's communication style.

6-7 points: HIGHLY ACCESSIBLE
- Articulate and clear expression throughout
- Minimal jargon or explains specialized terms
- Complex ideas communicated accessibly
- Strong verbal communication skills
- Little to no dialect that would impede comprehension
- Self-contextualizes references ("the NAACP, which is...")
- General audiences can easily follow

4-5 points: GENERALLY ACCESSIBLE
- Mostly clear communication
- Some jargon or unexplained acronyms
- Moderate dialect that may require familiarity
- Occasional unclear passages
- Some insider knowledge assumed but manageable

2-3 points: CHALLENGING ACCESSIBILITY
- Difficult to understand in places
- Heavy dialect or strong accent
- Extensive jargon without explanation
- Poor articulation or rambling
- Assumes extensive background knowledge

1 point: VERY DIFFICULT
- Very hard to understand
- Severe articulation issues
- Impenetrable jargon


SUB-DIMENSION 4.2: Well-Paced Narrative Flow (7 points)
--------------------------------------------------------

Assess the rhythm, momentum, and engagement sustainability of the narrative.

6-7 points: EXCELLENT PACING
- Strong narrative momentum throughout
- Good balance of action and reflection
- Varies pace (fast for dramatic moments, slower for analysis)
- Sustains attention from beginning to end
- Natural storytelling rhythm
- Interviewer facilitates without interrupting

4-5 points: GENERALLY GOOD PACING
- Generally maintains momentum
- Some strong sections, some slower
- Adequate variation in pacing
- Most of interview sustains attention
- Occasional lulls but recovers

2-3 points: UNEVEN PACING
- Frequently loses momentum
- Plodding or disjointed
- Poor variation (all one speed)
- Difficult to sustain attention
- Multiple slow or confusing sections

1 point: POOR PACING
- Cannot maintain momentum
- Extremely slow or chaotic
- Painful to sit through

Assess how well the interview links personal experience to larger Civil Rights themes and contemporary issues.

5-6 points: STRONG THEMATIC CONNECTIONS
- Explicitly connects personal story to broader movement themes
- Reflects on historical significance of experiences
- Draws clear connections to contemporary issues
- Situates personal experience in larger context
- Makes historical meaning accessible
- Helps audiences understand "so what?" and "why does this matter?"

3-4 points: MODERATE CONNECTIONS
- Some broader connections made
- Implicit rather than explicit themes
- Moderate contextualization
- Some reflection on larger meaning

1-2 points: LIMITED CONNECTIONS
- Entirely personal without broader context
- No reflection on larger meaning or significance
- Doesn't connect to themes beyond specific events

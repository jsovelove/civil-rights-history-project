## EXTRACTION PROCESS

### STEP 1: Read Entire Transcript
- Review ALL segments, not just early portions
- Note chapter structure and topic shifts
- Identify potential clip boundaries

### STEP 2: Identify Clip Candidates
- Look for natural story units (complete narratives)
- Mark segments where topic shifts occur
- Find high-engagement moments (drama, emotion, insight)
- Aim for **15-25 clips per 2-hour interview**

### Strategy: Expected Clip Count by Chapter (2-Hour Interview)

**Chapter 1-2 (0-20 min):** 2-4 clips
- Opening stories, family background
- Early experiences, formative moments

**Chapter 3-5 (20-60 min):** 5-8 clips
- Peak activism period
- Key events and organizing work
- Interactions with leaders

**Chapter 6-8 (60-100 min):** 5-8 clips
- Specific campaigns and events
- Dramatic incidents
- Organizational strategy

**Chapter 9-10 (100-120 min):** 3-5 clips
- Reflections on impact
- Contemporary connections
- Legacy and lessons

**If finding 25+ viable clips:** You're probably being too lenient on "standalone quality"
**If finding <10 viable clips:** You're probably being too strict - look for more moments

## CLIP IDENTIFICATION CRITERIA

### What Makes a VIABLE Clip?

**Minimum Requirements (must meet ALL):**
- ✅ Duration: 30 seconds to 10 minutes
- ✅ Clear start and end points in transcript
- ✅ At least ONE Main Topic tagged
- ✅ Total score ≥ 50/100
- ✅ Reasonably self-contained (score ≥7 on Accessibility)

**Ideal Characteristics (should meet MOST):**
- Duration: 2-5 minutes
- Addresses specific topic/event (not just general discussion)
- Contains vivid details (names, dates, places, sensory descriptions)
- Has narrative arc (beginning → middle → end)
- Emotionally engaging
- Natural boundaries (doesn't cut mid-thought)
- Minimal jargon or insider knowledge required

### What to EXTRACT:

**Personal Narratives** (Extract these!)
- "I remember when..." stories with specific details
- Dramatic incidents with tension and resolution
- First-person experiences of events
- Encounters with historical figures
- Moments of transformation or realization

**Historical Documentation** (Extract these!)
- Eyewitness accounts of key events
- Organizational strategy and decision-making
- Details of tactics and methods (protests, organizing, legal work)
- Behind-the-scenes movement activities
- Rare or unique testimony

**Reflective Wisdom** (Extract these!)
- Meaning-making about movement significance
- Contemporary connections to current struggles
- Analysis of successes and failures
- Long-term impact and legacy discussions
- Lessons learned over time

### What to SKIP:

**Procedural/Administrative** (Usually skip)
- Long organizational process descriptions without human story
- Dense policy explanations without personal connection
- Technical legal arguments without context

**Fragmented** (Skip)
- Incomplete thoughts that trail off
- Multiple false starts without payoff
- Requires extensive prior segments to understand

**Low-Quality** (Skip)
- Vague, general statements without specifics
- Rambling without clear point or structure
- Emotionally flat recitations of facts
